ANALYSIS: WHY THE DISCREPANCY?

### Hypothesis 1: DUPLICATES vs UNIQUE RECORDS
Claude chat may have been referring to UNIQUE case numbers, not total rows.

Testing:
- Actual Total Rows (2019-2025): 714,689
- Unique ReportNumberNew: Data shows ~76% uniqueness rate
- Estimated Unique (2019-2025): ~543,000 (still much higher than 214K)

❌ REJECTED: Even unique counts are far higher than expected

### Hypothesis 2: INCIDENT vs SUPPLEMENTAL RECORDS
CAD data includes both initial incidents and supplemental reports.
Expected counts may have been for incidents only, not supplements.

Supporting Evidence:
- ReportNumberNew pattern: "YY-NNNNNN" for incidents
- ReportNumberNew pattern: "YY-NNNNNN[A-Z]" for supplements (e.g., 25-000001A)
- Many events have multiple CAD records (units, status updates, supplements)

This explains the ~2-3x multiplier!

✓ LIKELY CAUSE: Expected counts were INCIDENT estimates, actual data includes
               all CAD events (initial + supplements + unit records)

### Hypothesis 3: DATA SOURCE CHANGED
Expected counts may have been from a different query or export type.

Supporting Evidence:
- File naming: "2019_CAD_ALL.xlsx" suggests comprehensive export
- Size analysis: Files are 2-3 MB each (consistent with detailed CAD logs)
- Current exports: Include ALL CAD activity, not just incident reports

✓ CONTRIBUTING FACTOR: Export includes complete CAD activity log

### Hypothesis 4: YEAR COUNT ERROR
